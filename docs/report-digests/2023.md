# Lake Quinsigamond Water Quality — 2023

**Overall State of the Lake: Good/Fair**

In 2023 Lake Quinsigamond received an overall 'Good/Fair' rating from the City of Worcester Lakes and Ponds Program. The lake was sampled semimonthly from May through October at seven sites: three tributaries, the northern and southern deep in-lake sites, and the Irish Dam outlet. Fecal bacteria were the season's main recreational concern: DCR closed Regatta Point beach for 63 days (two closure events, 18 tests) and Lake Park beach for 25 days (three closure events, ~17-18 tests), longer than 2021 and 2022, likely due to a very wet summer washing goose droppings into the water, earning a 'Fair' bacteria rating. Water clarity declined to a 'Fair' rating for the first time, with Secchi depths of 4.75-13.25 ft. Surface dissolved oxygen stayed 'Excellent' but bottom water became hypoxic after late May, producing three days when no part of the column met both 4 mg/L DO and 20C for cold-water fish ('the squeeze'). Surface phosphorus stayed very low while bottom phosphorus, especially at the southern site, rose to 0.388 mg/L. Cyanobacteria never reached bloom thresholds during the bathing season ('Good'), though fall scums were again reported. Herbicide treatment of invasive milfoil and fanwort in the northern lake was successful.

## Beaches

- **Regatta Point:** 63 days closed across 2 event(s); tested 18 times.
- **Lake Park:** 25 days closed across 3 event(s); tested 18 times.

## Key metrics

- **Water clarity (Secchi):** 4.75–13.25 ft
- **Peak deep-water total phosphorus (Southern site):** 0.388 mg/L
- **Max surface temperature:** 27.0 °C
- **Cold-water 'squeeze' days (no habitable layer):** 3
- **Fish kills:** None reported; risk noted from 'the squeeze' (habitat compression for cold-water fish)

## Key findings

- Overall 'Good/Fair' State of the Lake rating for 2023.
- Beach closures were the season's main concern: Regatta Point closed 63 days (2 events) and Lake Park closed 25 days (3 events), longer than 2021-2022, attributed to a record-wet summer washing goose droppings into the water; bacteria rated 'Fair'.
- Water clarity declined to a 'Fair' rating for the first time, with Secchi depths of 4.75-13.25 ft and most readings 4-10 ft.
- Strong summer stratification drove bottom-water hypoxia (bottom DO below 4 mg/L after 24-May, as low as 0.07 mg/L); on 3 days (14-Aug, 24-Aug, 16-Sep) no part of the column met both 4 mg/L DO and 20C, 'squeezing' cold-water fish habitat.
- Surface phosphorus stayed very low ('Excellent'), but bottom phosphorus rose over the season, peaking at 0.388 mg/L at the southern site; cyanobacteria stayed below bloom thresholds during the bathing season ('Good') though fall scums recurred.

## Notable events

- **Herbicide treatment (invasive plants) (2023-08-23)** — Contracted chemical treatment of Eurasian Milfoil, Variable Milfoil, and Fanwort in the Northern section of the lake using Flumigard SC (Flumioxazin) and ProcellaCOR (Florpyrauxifen-benzyl).
- **Water Chestnut hand-pulling event (2023-07)** — LQWA organized a volunteer hand-pulling event; volunteers worked from kayaks and small boats to clear Water Chestnut patches.
- **Heavy rain / wet-weather sampling days (2023 (27-Jun, 11-Jul, 26-Jul, 12-Sep))** — Second-wettest summer on record in Worcester. Wet-weather sampling: 27-Jun 0.81 in, 11-Jul 2.50 in, 26-Jul 0.38 in, 12-Sep 2.50 in of rain in prior 24 hours.
- **First mid-lake bacteria exceedance (LQWA) (2023)** — LQWA tributary E. coli program recorded 1046.2 MPN/100 mL at the North Basin mid-point.
- **Text message alert system launch (2023)** — L&P launched a text alert system notifying residents of beach closures and boat-ramp closures for invasive plant treatments.

## Caveats

- Internal inconsistency on Lake Park beach testing count: page 5 text says 'tested 18 times' while the Table 1 caption says 'sampled 17 times'. Days closed (25) and closures (3) agree.
- The report mislabels the Dissolved Oxygen rating as 'Temperature' on page 10 ('L&P rated Temperature ... as Fair'); context and the DO section indicate this 'Fair' applies to Dissolved Oxygen, while Temperature is rated 'Good' on page 8.
- PFAS and industrial contaminants were NOT tested in 2023 (tested on a 3-year cycle, last in 2022). The schema's pfas MCL field (20 ng/L) is not referenced anywhere in this 2023 report; belowMCL is null.
- Cyanobacteria is reported via phycocyanin (ug/L), not cell density (cells/mL); no peak cells/mL value exists, so peakDensity is null and unit reflects the reported phycocyanin metric.
- No letter ratings are stated in the text for pH, nutrients, invasive species, tributaries, or litter; these rating fields are left null rather than guessed.
- Seasonal line-chart values (Secchi, temperature, DO, TP, phycocyanin) are read approximately from figures; only text-stated min/max/peak values are high confidence. Depth-profile series list sampling dates only (per-depth values not numerically transcribable from the small appendix plots).
- Several figure ranges quoted in figure captions differ slightly from body text (e.g., Fig 3 caption 4.75-12.25 ft vs body 4.75-13.25 ft combined); body-text combined ranges used for top-level metrics.
