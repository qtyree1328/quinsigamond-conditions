# Lake Quinsigamond Water Quality — 2025

**Overall State of the Lake: Good**

In 2025, the City of Worcester's Lakes and Ponds Program rated Lake Quinsigamond's overall State of the Lake as "Good." Unlike recent seasons, fecal bacteria did not significantly limit recreation: DCR tested the Lake Park and Regatta Point beaches 15 times, and Lake Park closed only once for 7 days in late May, while Regatta Point never closed. Water clarity was rated "Good," with Secchi depth between 7.25 and 14.75 ft (most readings in the "Fair" 4-10 ft band). Surface water reached up to 28.5 C, producing strong summer stratification; bottom temperatures stayed near 3.8-5.9 C. Surface dissolved oxygen was "Excellent" (7.70-11.22 mg/L) but bottom DO was "Poor" (0.09-2.35 mg/L), squeezing cold-water fish habitat and earning a "Fair" DO rating. Surface phosphorus stayed "Excellent," but bottom TP rose sharply, peaking at 0.673 mg/L at the Southern Site. Cyanobacteria stayed below bloom thresholds (rated "Good"). Multiple invasive aquatic plants persist, managed via diver harvesting, volunteer Water Chestnut pulls, and prior herbicide treatment.

## Key metrics

- **Peak deep-water total phosphorus (Southern site):** 0.673 mg/L
- **Max surface temperature:** 28.5 °C
- **Cold-water 'squeeze' days (no habitable layer):** 2
- **Fish kills:** No fish kills reported; cold-water fish habitat 'squeeze' described, with only a 5-10 ft habitable band between 16-Jul and 8-Oct.

## Key findings

- Overall State of the Lake rated 'Good' for 2025, an improvement over 2024's 'Good/Fair'.
- Fecal bacteria barely affected recreation: only one closure (Lake Park, 7 days, late May); Regatta Point never closed.
- Strong summer stratification produced 'Poor' bottom dissolved oxygen (0.09-2.35 mg/L) and squeezed cold-water fish habitat to a 5-10 ft band, yielding a 'Fair' DO rating.
- Bottom total phosphorus rose sharply over the season, peaking at 0.673 mg/L at the Southern Site, while surface TP stayed 'Excellent'.
- Cyanobacteria remained below bloom thresholds (phycocyanin never above 50 ug/L); rated 'Good' with no beach closures.
- Invasive plant management continued; 2023 herbicide treatment confirmed successful with Eurasian Milfoil reduced to two small patches near Ramshorn Island.

## Notable events

- **Lake Park Beach closure (Late May 2025)** — Lake Park Beach closed for 7 days following an Enterococcus single-day maximum exceedance.
- **Fanwort removal via Diver-Assisted Suction Harvesting (DASH) (August 2025)** — L&P contracted divers to remove Fanwort in the Round Pond section, where it was densely intermixed with endangered Vasey's Pondweed.
- **Water Chestnut volunteer removal events (June-July 2025)** — LQWA and L&P co-hosted two volunteer hand-pulling events north of Route 70 / Lincoln Street using canoes and kayaks.
- **Particle separator installation (Completed late 2025)** — Two particle separators installed in stormwater drainage lines discharging into Lake Quinsigamond (e.g., Suntaug Road), funded by ARPA.
- **Professional aquatic plant survey (August 2025)** — Mapped extents of Fanwort, Eurasian Milfoil, Variable Leaf Milfoil, Brittle Naiad, Water Chestnut, and Common Reed plus overall plant cover.
- **Watershed-based planning public workshops (April 2025)** — Public workshops gathered community input on pollutant sources to guide field assessments for stormwater control measures.

## Caveats

- COPY-PASTE ERROR: The report's Background (page 1) states it 'will also outline projects and opportunities the City of Worcester's Lakes and Ponds Program (L&P) intends to implement at Indian Lake in 2026' - 'Indian Lake' appears to be an erroneous reference; the report is about Lake Quinsigamond. All data reported here are for Lake Quinsigamond.
- Section header inconsistencies: 'Ongoing Projects and Plan for 2025' (page 17) describes 2026 plans; body text repeatedly references 2026 activities.
- Per-site seasonal averages for Secchi clarity and total phosphorus are not stated numerically in the report; only ranges, maxima, and rating bands are given. 'avg' and per-site 'northAvg/southAvg' fields are null rather than guessed.
- Time-series values for line-chart figures (Secchi, temperature, DO, pH, TP, phycocyanin) were approximated from page images (medium/low confidence). Text-stated min/max/peak values are high confidence and should be preferred.
- Bottom in-lake pH: body text (page 11) states 6.62-6.84, but the Figures 14-15 caption states 6.62-7.73. The wider 6.62-7.73 range is used for the metric max; min is 6.62.
- Cyanobacteria peak density is reported as relative-abundance categories ('None' to 'High'), not numeric cells/mL; peakDensity is null.
- No PFAS or industrial-contaminant testing occurred in 2025 (rescheduled to spring 2026); the most recent data are from 2022 with 'no results of concern'. No numeric PFAS values or MCL are stated in the 2025 report; pfas fields reflect this.
- pH, nutrients, invasiveSpecies, tributaries, and litter do not receive an explicit single-word 'State of the Lake' rating in the 2025 report text; those rating fields are null.
- Central Massachusetts experienced fluctuating 2025 drought (Level 2-3 early, Normal in summer, Level 2 in Sep-Oct), reducing tributary flow; Poor Farm Brook was dry on multiple sampling occasions, limiting tributary data.
