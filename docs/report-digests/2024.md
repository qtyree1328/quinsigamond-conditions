# Lake Quinsigamond Water Quality — 2024

**Overall State of the Lake: Good/Fair**

In 2024, the City of Worcester's Lakes and Ponds Program rated Lake Quinsigamond "Good/Fair." The largest impediment to recreation was closure of the two DCR state-park beaches due to fecal bacteria (Enterococcus) exceedances: Regatta Point Beach was closed 50 days and Lake Park Beach 24 days, earning a "Fair" rating for fecal bacteria. Water clarity improved over 2023, with Secchi depth between 6.75 and 13.25 feet (mostly "Good"), rated "Good" overall. Surface dissolved oxygen stayed "Excellent" all season, but strong summer thermal stratification depleted bottom oxygen and squeezed cold-water fish habitat to a narrow 5-10 foot band between mid-July and mid-September, yielding a "Fair" DO rating. Temperature was rated "Good." Surface nutrients were very low, though bottom total phosphorus and ammonia rose sharply as stratification intensified (southern bottom TP peaked at 0.509 mg/L). Cyanobacteria stayed below bloom thresholds during the swimming season (rated "Good"), with elevated risk on 27-Jul and 5-Aug. Multiple invasive aquatic plants persist; management and volunteer Water Chestnut removal continued. Two continuous monitoring buoys were deployed.

## Beaches

- **Regatta Point:** 50 days closed across 2 event(s); tested 18 times.
- **Lake Park:** 24 days closed across 3 event(s); tested 19 times.

## Key metrics

- **Water clarity (Secchi):** 6.75–13.25 ft
- **Peak deep-water total phosphorus (Southern site):** 0.509 mg/L
- **Max surface temperature:** 28.4 °C
- **Fish kills:** No fish kills reported in 2024.

## Key findings

- Overall rating Good/Fair; beach closures from fecal bacteria were the largest impediment to recreation (Regatta Point 50 days, Lake Park 24 days).
- Water clarity improved over 2023 (Secchi 6.75-13.25 ft, mostly Good); rated Good overall.
- Strong summer thermal stratification (max surface-bottom difference 22.9C on 18-Jul) depleted bottom dissolved oxygen, squeezing cold-water fish to a 5-10 ft habitat band from mid-July to mid-September; DO rated Fair.
- Surface nutrients were very low (surface TP <0.025 mg/L), but bottom total phosphorus (southern max 0.509 mg/L) and ammonia rose sharply with stratification.
- Cyanobacteria stayed below bloom thresholds during the swimming season (rated Good), with elevated risk only on 27-Jul and 5-Aug; fall ephemeral scums reported in the southern lake.

## Notable events

- **Northern-section herbicide treatment follow-up survey (Early June 2024)** — L&P surveyed the 31-acre area treated with herbicide in 2023 (Eurasian Milfoil, Variable Milfoil, Fanwort) to assess efficacy.
- **Volunteer Water Chestnut removal events (June-July 2024)** — LQWA and a Girl Scout Gold Award candidate organized three hand-pulling events (two at Gauch Park, one at Sunset Beach), with L&P-loaned City kayaks.
- **Fall cyanobacteria scum reports (Late September-fall 2024)** — Residents reported ephemeral scums in the southern portion of the lake earlier than usual; L&P responded to several reports.
- **Wet-weather sampling day (9-May-2024)** — 0.62 inches of rain in the 24 hours prior to sampling; categorized as wet weather. 21 of the visits had <0.25 inches in the prior 24 hours.
- **Level-3 Critical drought (1-Oct-2024 through 8-Jan-2025)** — MA Central Region classified Level-3 Critical drought; fall 2024 was the driest in the period of record. Downgraded to Level-2 on 8-Jan-2025.

## Caveats

- Page 1 contains an apparent copy-paste error stating the report will outline projects 'at Indian Lake in 2025'; the rest of the report is about Lake Quinsigamond.
- Beach single-sample bacteria values (pages 22-23) were read visually from bar charts and are approximate; exact dates on the Lake Park chart were partly illegible, so some date labels are inferred.
- Cyanobacteria 'peakDensity' in cells/mL is null because the report uses relative density categories (none/low/high), not absolute counts.
- No PFAS or industrial-contaminant testing occurred in 2024 (last done 2022, next planned 2025); no PFAS numeric value or MCL is stated in this report, so the schema's '20 ng/L' MCL is a placeholder not sourced from the 2024 document.
- The 2024 buoys produced no time-series figure in the report (one buoy detached in July); Figure 22 is a photograph, not buoy data.
- Ratings for pH, nutrients, invasiveSpecies, tributaries, and litter are null because the report does not assign discrete Excellent/Good/Fair/Poor State-of-the-Lake ratings to those individual categories.
- Secchi and most metric averages are not stated numerically in the text; only ranges/peaks are reported, so 'avg' fields are null rather than estimated.
- The Page 2 species list includes Sacred Lotus and Sacred Lotus is omitted from the Page 16 managed-species list; both are reported as present.
