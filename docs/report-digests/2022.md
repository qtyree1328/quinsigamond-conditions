# Lake Quinsigamond Water Quality — 2022

**Overall State of the Lake: Good**

In 2022, the City of Worcester's Lakes and Ponds Program rated the overall state of Lake Quinsigamond as "Good," the same as 2021. Fecal bacteria challenges were localized to the shoreline: the Regatta Point beach was closed 32 days across four exceedance events (tested 21 times), while Lake Park had no closures (tested 15 times), and open-water E. coli stayed low. Water clarity decreased from 2021, with Secchi depths of 6.75 to 17 feet, mostly in the "Fair" range. Surface water reached a maximum of 28.3 C in early August, about 0.9 C warmer than 2021. The lake stratified by late June, and bottom dissolved oxygen fell below 4 mg/L (hypoxic) after June 29; on August 24 and September 13 no portion of the water column met both the 4 mg/L oxygen and 20 C temperature thresholds ("the squeeze"), though no fish kills occurred. Surface phosphorus stayed very low, but bottom phosphorus at the southern site rose sharply, peaking at 0.408 mg/L on October 26. No summer cyanobacteria blooms were observed, but residents reported fall scums earlier than usual. PFAS and other contaminants remained below health thresholds. Two notable disturbances occurred: a 5.75-million-gallon sewage overflow in February and repeated construction sediment runoff.

## Beaches

- **Regatta Point:** 32 days closed across 4 event(s); tested 21 times.
- **Lake Park:** 0 days closed across 0 event(s); tested 15 times.

## Key metrics

- **Water clarity (Secchi):** 6.75–17.0 ft
- **Peak deep-water total phosphorus (Southern site):** 0.408 mg/L
- **Max surface temperature:** 28.3 °C
- **Cold-water 'squeeze' days (no habitable layer):** 2
- **Fish kills:** no - no fish kills reported in 2022 despite stressful conditions
- **PFAS:** 13.3 ng/L (8/24/2022) and 16.13 ng/L (7/27/2022) (MCL 20 ng/L; below MCL: True)

## Key findings

- Overall state rated "Good" for 2022, unchanged from 2021.
- Regatta Point beach closed 32 days over 4 events; Lake Park had no closures; open-water bacteria stayed low.
- Dissolved oxygen rated "Fair" - deep-water hypoxia after 29-Jun and two "squeeze" days (24-Aug, 13-Sep), but no fish kills.
- Bottom total phosphorus rose sharply at the southern site, peaking at 0.408 mg/L on 26-Oct, while surface TP stayed very low.
- Two major disturbances: a 5.75-million-gallon February sewage overflow and repeated Belmont Street construction sediment runoff (outfall TP 4.66 mg/L).

## Notable events

- **Lake Ave Sewer Pump Station Overflow (2022-02-06)** — A pump at the Worcester Sewer Pump Station on Lake Ave catastrophically failed, flooding the building and overflowing 5.75 million gallons of untreated sewage over 36 hours into Lake Quinsigamond south of the Route 9 bridge. DIS issued a Recreational Contact Advisory; L&P conducted targeted monitoring over two months.
- **Belmont Street Construction Sediment Runoff (2020-10 to 2022-04 (sampled 25-Mar-2022))** — During heavy rainfall, sediment plumes emanated from the Route 9 stormwater outfall, traced to active construction sites along Belmont Street and Hospital Drive with exposed soils. Occurred a minimum of six times between Oct 2020 and Apr 2022. On 25-Mar-2022 L&P sampled a visible plume.

## Caveats

- No statistical analysis was performed; raw data only. Ratings use MassDEP SMART Monitoring Watershed Report Card Criteria.
- Long probe cable difficulties prevented collection of bottom temperature and DO data after 24-Aug.
- An equipment failure on 18-Sep prevented DO readings below 25 ft for the rest of the season.
- Temperature/DO depth readings were limited to 65 ft after 15-Jun due to equipment issues.
- Bottom pH: only 5 of 12 planned data points were viable (cable issues plus two censored datasets), limiting conclusions.
- Ammonia (NH3) data were excluded due to extensive QAQC issues at Alpha Analytical Laboratory.
- Drought (Level 3 Critical, July-August) left Coal Mine Brook and Poor Farm Brook without enough water to sample for 3 and 4 events respectively; 2022 was the longest L&P has observed Coal Mine Brook with no flow.
- Cyanobacteria density reported as relative/qualitative (low to high), not as absolute cells/mL counts.
- Tracking transient fall cyanobacteria scum reports across the large lake is difficult given limited L&P staffing.
- Secchi readings near the Route 9 outfall were generally obscured by heavy aquatic plant growth.
